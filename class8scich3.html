Class 8 Science – Chapter 3

Synthetic Fibres and Plastics

1. Explain why some fibres are called synthetic.

Fibres made by humans using chemical processes are called synthetic fibres because they are not obtained from natural sources.

2. Mark the correct answer:

Rayon is different from synthetic fibres because it is obtained from wood pulp.
Answer: (c) wood pulp

3. Fill in the blanks:

(a) Nylon
(b) Polyester
(c) Plastic
(d) Thermosetting

4. Give examples of thermoplastics and thermosetting plastics.

Thermoplastics: PVC, polythene
Thermosetting plastics: Bakelite, melamine

5. Explain why plastic containers are favored for storing food.

Plastics are lightweight, non-reactive to food, do not rust, and are durable, making them ideal for food storage.

6. Explain the difference between thermoplastics and thermosetting plastics.

Thermoplastics can be melted and reshaped multiple times.
Thermosetting plastics can only be molded once and do not soften on heating.

7. Explain why handles of frying pans are made of thermosetting plastics.

Thermosetting plastics like bakelite do not melt on heating and are poor conductors of heat, making them ideal for pan handles.

8. Categorize materials as biodegradable and non-biodegradable:

Biodegradable: Paper, cotton cloth, woolen cloth, vegetable peels
Non-biodegradable: Plastic, aluminum can, polythene bag

9. How are synthetic fibres more durable than natural fibres?

Synthetic fibres are strong, elastic, lightweight, and resistant to chemicals and wrinkles, making them more durable than natural fibres.

10. Why should we minimize the use of plastics?

Plastics are non-biodegradable and cause environmental pollution. They also harm animals if ingested and are difficult to dispose of safely.
