4. Can microorganisms be seen with the naked eye?

No, they are too small and can only be seen using a microscope.

5. What are microorganisms? Give four examples.

They are tiny organisms not visible to the naked eye. Examples: Bacteria, Fungi, Protozoa, Virus.

6. Classify microorganisms:

Bacteria, Fungi, Protozoa, Algae, Virus

7. Role of microorganisms in curd formation:

Lactobacillus bacteria convert milk into curd by producing lactic acid.

8. Commercial use of microorganisms:

Used to make alcohol, wine, vinegar, and baking bread using yeast.

9. How do microorganisms help in nitrogen fixation?

Rhizobium bacteria live in root nodules and convert atmospheric nitrogen into a form plants can absorb.

10. How are antibiotics produced?

They are chemicals produced by microorganisms like fungi and bacteria that kill or stop the growth of other microbes.

11. Harmful effects of microorganisms:

Cause diseases in humans, animals, and plants; spoil food; make water unfit to drink.

12. Food preservation methods:

Boiling, refrigeration, adding salt or sugar, using preservatives, and vacuum sealing.

13. How do vaccines work?

Weakened microbes are introduced to trigger immune response, creating memory cells to fight future infections.

14. What do you mean by nitrogen cycle?

It is the process of converting nitrogen into different forms for use by plants and animals, and returning it to the atmosphere.
