1. Which of the following can be beaten into thin sheets?

Answer: (a) Zinc (because it is a metal)

2. Which of the following statements is correct?

Answer: (b) All metals are ductile (Note: Technically, not all metals, but this is the correct NCERT answer.)

5. Some properties of metals are listed below. Which one is not correct?

Answer: (d) Metals are poor conductors of heat and electricity (This is incorrect; metals are good conductors.)

6. Explain why copper is used to make electric wires.

Copper is a good conductor of electricity and is ductile, making it suitable for wire drawing.

7. Why is sodium stored in kerosene?

Sodium is a very reactive metal. It reacts vigorously with air and water, so it is stored in kerosene to prevent these reactions.

8. Can you store lemon pickle in an aluminium utensil? Explain.

No, because acids in lemon pickle react with aluminium and form harmful compounds.

10. Saloni took a piece of burning charcoal and collected the gas evolved in a test tube. How will she find the nature of the gas?

She can dissolve the gas in water and test it with blue litmus paper. If it turns red, the gas is acidic in nature (carbon dioxide).

11. One day Reeta went to a jeweller’s shop with her mother. Her mother gave an old gold jewellery to the goldsmith to polish. Next day, they went to collect it and found that the gold lost a bit of its weight. Can you suggest a reason?

The gold was dipped in a solution of acid to remove impurities. The acid also dissolved some gold, hence the weight loss.
